The Data Layer

Every signal below is captured passively, reduced to numbers on the device, and tracked against the patient's own baseline. Raw video and audio never leave the home — only de-identified time-series cross the wire. A knowledge-graph layer links those series to clinical concepts to produce an explainable decision suggestion, not a diagnosis.

Each series is emitted as a FHIR Observation so it slots straight into an EHR — plus a Fall & Triage Event log and an Activity-Rhythm deviation series feeding the same timeline.

🔒Privacy boundary. Raw video & audio never leave the device. Only de-identified numeric telemetry crosses into the cloud — which is why cloud analysis and LLM summarisation are safe here.
04Temporal fusion & knowledge graphwhere separate signals become one clinical picture

Method: align all streams on one timeline → model a personal baseline → detect sustained change with CUSUM / change-point → measure lagged cross-correlation between dose timing and each symptom → walk the graph from a flagged change to its linked clinical concept and guideline → hand the linked evidence to an LLM for a plain-language summary.

05Routed outputswellbeing to family · pathology to clinician

Family — peace of mind

"Arthur's day looks normal." Adherence calendar + alert history with triage transcript. No clinical metrics.

Clinician — 1-page report + exception alert

Gait & voice trends plotted against dose timing. Alert fires only on sustained threshold crossings, into the existing EHR queue.

Decision support

"Flag for Review" with the graph's reasoning attached. The clinician decides — the system never auto-prescribes or diagnoses.

Two stores, on purpose. Time-series DB holds the signals; the graph DB holds the relationships and clinical concepts that make a suggestion explainable. If you meant TerminusDB by "Turing DB," it fits the graph slot (its version-control is a bonus for auditability); confirm the product name before it goes on a slide.

Honest caveats to state before a judge does. rPPG heart rate is trend-grade, motion/light-sensitive, and has documented accuracy bias across skin tones — a signal, not a vitals monitor. Ambient sleep staging needs radar or a wearable. Everything here produces trend signals feeding clinician decision support, not diagnostic-grade measurements.
